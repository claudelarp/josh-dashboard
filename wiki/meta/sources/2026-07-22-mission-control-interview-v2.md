# Mission Control Scoping Interview v2 (blank) + Fable 5 Synthesis Prompt

A 9-section scoping interview written specifically for Josh's situation (names Blossom, McIntire, the eleven skills, the voiceover-vs-text-overlay question) — **currently unanswered** — plus the synthesis prompt that turns a completed interview into a build spec.

## Key takeaways

- Section 1 (task inventory: a week of end-of-day logging, marked R/H/I/J) is declared the most important section — every skill must trace to a line in it. Section 4 (voice capture) requires pasting real artifacts verbatim, not describing them.
- Section 0 is disqualifiers: three concrete recent drops, the abandonment history of past systems, what's already opened daily (the real substrate), and 0.6 — whether building this is avoidance of the actual highest-value thing.
- Sections 2–3 (connection map, data provenance) force per-metric honesty: where does the number live, can it be pulled, and "how many consecutive days will you actually type it in."
- Sections 5–6 (decision rights, cadence) demand per-tool permission answers and rank scheduled slots within a plan budget.
- Section 7 applies per-domain scrutiny, including arguing *against* including self-development at all.
- Section 9 requires a written kill criterion, dated, before emotional investment in the build.
- The synthesis prompt's inputs name **Claude Code + an Obsidian vault** as the target runtime — newer intent than the PRD's Cowork targeting.
